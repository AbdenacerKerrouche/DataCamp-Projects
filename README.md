## 🗂️ Projects

### 🔍 [Analyzing Crime in Los Angeles](./Analyzing-Crime-in-Los-Angeles/)

**Domain:** Public Safety · **Skills:** Data Wrangling, EDA, Time Analysis

Los Angeles attracts people from all over the world — not all opportunities are positive ones. In this project, I acted as a data detective supporting the LAPD in analyzing crime data to guide resource allocation.

**Key questions answered:**
- Which hour of the day has the highest frequency of crimes?
- Which area has the largest frequency of night crimes (10 PM – 3:59 AM)?
- How are crimes distributed across victim age groups?

**Tools used:** `pandas`, `matplotlib`

---

### 🏫 [Exploring NYC Public School Test Result Scores](./Exploring%20NYC%20Public%20School%20Test%20Result%20Scores/)

**Domain:** Education · **Skills:** Aggregation, Statistical Analysis, Ranking

Standardized test results shape the futures of millions of students. This project explores SAT performance data from NYC public schools to surface top performers and uncover borough-level patterns.

**Key questions answered:**
- Which schools have the best math results (≥ 80% of max score)?
- What are the top 10 schools by combined SAT score?
- Which borough has the highest standard deviation in SAT performance?

**Tools used:** `pandas`

---

### 🎬 [Investigating Netflix Movies](./Investigating%20Netflix%20Movies/)

**Domain:** Entertainment · **Skills:** EDA, Filtering, Data Visualization

A production company wants insights into Netflix movies from the 1990s. This project explores trends in movie duration and genre from that iconic decade.

**Key questions answered:**
- What was the most frequent movie duration in the 1990s?
- How many short (< 90 min) action movies were released in that decade?

**Tools used:** `pandas`, `matplotlib`

---

### 🏅 [Visualizing the History of Nobel Prize Winners](./Visualizing%20the%20History%20of%20Nobel%20Prize%20Winners/)

**Domain:** History & Science · **Skills:** Data Visualization, Pattern Analysis, Grouping

The Nobel Prize has been awarded since 1901. Are there biases in who receives it? This project uses data manipulation and visualization to explore over a century of Nobel Prize history.

**Key questions answered:**
- What is the most commonly awarded gender and birth country?
- Which decade had the highest ratio of US-born winners?
- Which decade/category combination had the highest proportion of female laureates?
- Who was the first woman to win, and in what category?
- Which individuals or organizations have won more than once?

**Tools used:** `pandas`, `matplotlib`, `seaborn`
